About Me

I am a Ph.D. Candidate in Public Policy and Political Economy (Health Economics) at the University of Texas at Dallas, expected to graduate in Spring 2026.

My research lies at the intersection of Behavioral Economics and Public Health. I specialize in using Discrete Choice Experiments (DCEs) and structural modeling to understand intertemporal decision-making, particularly how individuals value delayed health protection such as vaccination.

Currently, I am working on estimating behavioral discount rates for vaccination decisions and exploring how these insights can inform equity-sensitive health policies. My work has been supported by the UTD Dean of Graduate Education Dissertation Research Award.
