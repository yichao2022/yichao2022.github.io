1. Vision: Generative Population Modeling

As a Problem Solver bridging economic theory and machine learning, I propose the use of Behavioral Digital Twins (BDT) to address the complexity of modern tobacco use.

Traditional models often fail to capture the granular, non-linear transitions between traditional cigarettes, E-cigarettes, and HTPs (like IQOS). My approach creates individual-level generative agents that “experience” policy changes in a simulated environment before they manifest in the population.

2. Core Research Synergies

NoteBDT Logic Flow: From Data to Policy Audit

A. Modeling Transitions & Poly-use Dynamics

Transitions are rarely binary. My BDT framework treats a user’s tobacco journey as a stochastic state-space model. - Product Attributes: I model how flavor bans or nicotine caps specifically alter the “utility surface” for users, driving them toward either cessation or alternative product uptake (e.g., switching from E-cigs back to combustibles). - Dynamic Poly-use: Simulation of concurrent use patterns (Cigarette + Vaping) based on real-world longitudinal survey data.

B. Precision Equity: Vulnerable Group Simulations

Aggregated data masks inequality. I specialize in disaggregated agent modeling for: - Low SES & Minority Groups: Integrating specific behavioral constraints (financial burden, retail density) to predict how tax increases or marketing restrictions affect vulnerable groups differently than the general population. - Youth & Social Media Influence: Utilizing ML to extract behavioral priors from social media (e.g., TikTok/XHS) to model the “initiation” phase for adolescents.

C. Counter-Industry Predictive Modeling: The Independent Audit Tool

In response to industry-funded health impact models (e.g., PMI’s population models), I advocate for Independent, Transparent, and Robust Population Health Simulations.

  • Logic Audit: Applying “System 2” reasoning (via DeepSeek/Claude) to audit the causal assumptions in industry models.
  • Future Forecasting: Using BDTs to provide evidence-based projections for FDA/State-level regulatory impact assessments.

3. Technical Stack for UCSF

  • Generative Agents: Large-scale simulation of 100k+ independent tobacco-using agents.
  • ML & Social Media: NLP pipelines for analyzing tobacco/cannabis sentiment and transition signals from unstandardized data.
  • Large Data Economics: Expertise in longitudinal survey analysis (PATH, NHIS) integrated with structural modeling.

4. Proposed Roadmap at IHPS

  1. Phase 1: Pilot a BDT model using California-specific tobacco purchase data to simulate the impact of recent flavor restrictions on Low SES households.
  2. Phase 2: Integrate social media sentiment analysis into transition probability matrices for emerging products (HTPs/Cannabis).
  3. Goal: Establish a “Regulatory Sandbox” for policy evaluation that prioritizes health equity and independent oversight.

### A. Modeling Transitions & Poly-use Dynamics
Transitions are rarely binary. My BDT framework treats a user's tobacco journey as a **stochastic state-space model**.
- **Product Attributes**: I model how flavor bans or nicotine caps specifically alter the "utility surface" for users, driving them toward either cessation or alternative product uptake (e.g., switching from E-cigs back to combustibles).
- **Dynamic Poly-use**: Simulation of concurrent use patterns (Cigarette + Vaping) based on real-world longitudinal survey data.

### B. Precision Equity: Vulnerable Group Simulations
Aggregated data masks inequality. I specialize in **disaggregated agent modeling** for:
- **Low SES & Minority Groups**: Integrating specific behavioral constraints (financial burden, retail density) to predict how tax increases or marketing restrictions affect vulnerable groups differently than the general population.
- **Youth & Social Media Influence**: Utilizing ML to extract behavioral priors from social media (e.g., TikTok/XHS) to model the "initiation" phase for adolescents.

### C. Counter-Industry Predictive Modeling: The Independent Audit Tool

In response to industry-funded health impact models (e.g., PMI's population models), I advocate for **Independent, Transparent, and Robust Population Health Simulations**.

| Feature | Industry "Black-Box" Models | Behavioral Digital Twin (BDT) |
|:---|:---:|:---:|
| **Transparency** | Proprietary / Hidden Causal Chains | **Open-Source / Peer-Audited** |
| **Granularity** | Aggregate cohorts | **Individual-level Agents** |
| **Bias Mitigation** | Optimized for commercial validation | **Optimized for Health Equity** |
| **Data Fusion** | Limited to historical trends | **Real-time Social Media + Survey Fusion** |

- **Logic Audit**: Applying "System 2" reasoning (via DeepSeek/Claude) to audit the causal assumptions in industry models.
- **Future Forecasting**: Using BDTs to provide evidence-based projections for FDA/State-level regulatory impact assessments.
