# Health-Incentive-Simulator: Precision Behavioral Interventions in Clinical Settings

**Target Audience:** Medical School PIs, Health Behavior Experts, Clinical Trial Directors (e.g., UCSF, Penn CHIBE).
**Core Narrative:** "Why do standard behavioral 'nudges' often fail in real-world clinical settings? Moving from static nudges to dynamic behavioral digital twins."

## 1. The Clinical Bottleneck: Intervention Fatigue
Despite the abundance of EHR data and digital health tools, patient non-adherence and clinical trial attrition remain massive challenges. Traditional behavioral economics relies on static "nudges" (e.g., text reminders, generic financial incentives), assuming uniform and stable patient responses. 

However, real-world patients experience **Cognitive Fatigue ($\kappa$)** and **Trust Depreciation ($\delta$)** over time. A nudge that works on day 1 often triggers avoidance by day 14.

## 2. The TRIBE-v2 Clinical Solution
The **Trust-Reinforced Intertemporal Behavioral Engine (TRIBE-v2)** acts as a dynamic "Digital Twin" for patient behavior. By bridging structural econometrics with digital health data, TRIBE-v2 provides:

* **Dynamic Parameterization:** We extract individual-level behavioral parameters (time preference $\beta$, trust capital $\delta$, cognitive cost $\kappa$) directly from longitudinal EHR, patient portal interactions, and wearable data.
* **Counterfactual Simulation:** Before launching an expensive clinical trial or adherence campaign, TRIBE-v2 simulates how different patient subgroups (especially the highly sensitive "marginal compliers") will react to varying incentive structures.
* **Precision Dose-Response:** We quantify the exact "behavioral cost" of compliance. This allows us to optimize the timing, frequency, and magnitude of interventions—maximizing patient retention while minimizing intervention budget and fatigue.

## 3. Strategic Integration for Your Lab
* **Optimizing Clinical Trial Retention:** Predicting which patient cohorts require structured financial incentives versus purely informational support to remain in long-term studies.
* **Chronic Disease Management:** Designing adaptive SMS or app-based intervention schedules that self-adjust based on predicted fatigue levels, avoiding the "notification blind-spot."
* **Health Equity & Trust:** Identifying vulnerable subgroups whose trust in the medical system can be effectively rebuilt through targeted, low-cognitive-burden interventions without backfiring.
